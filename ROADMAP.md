## What This Product Actually Is

Cognition OS is three things fused into one. No product on earth does all three together.

**One — ChatGPT-level chat capability.** Answers any question. Solves problems step by step. Explains concepts multiple ways until it clicks. Handles photos of questions. Reads uploaded PDFs. Writes study guides, revision sheets, practice tests, plans, summaries — all produced inline inside the conversation. The chat is a canvas. Everything a student needs comes out of one conversation window. They never leave the chat to get something done.

**Two — A companion that knows you completely.** Not surface-level personalisation like "Hello Akshat." The AI knows their exam, their deadline, their weak areas, their mistake history, their learning style, their emotional patterns, when they burn out, what analogies land for them, what they studied yesterday, what they are about to forget. Every single answer is shaped by who this specific student is and what they specifically need right now. ChatGPT gives a great answer. Cognition OS gives the right answer for this person at this moment.

**Three — A silent OS running underneath.** Six engines running continuously beneath the conversation. Tracking knowledge. Scheduling reviews. Planning days. Analysing mock tests. Watching mental state. The student never navigates to these. They never see module names. They just talk — and the product gets eerily accurate about their needs over time because the OS is watching everything and feeding the conversation silently.

The student experiences one thing: a companion who knows them. The OS is the engine. The companion is the product.

## The Problem — Why This Needs To Exist

Students are failing not because they do not study. They are failing because of five specific research-confirmed reasons no tool today solves completely.

**One — Passive learning feels productive but builds nothing.** Students spend hours rereading notes, highlighting textbooks, watching lectures. Research from 225 STEM courses confirms students in passive learning environments are 1.5 times more likely to fail. Feeling like you understand something is not proof that you do.

**Two — Memorisation without understanding.** Students memorise formulas and definitions. Exams test application. When a question is twisted or applied differently, memorisation collapses entirely. Familiarity is not mastery.

**Three — Broken foundations.** Students attempt advanced topics while foundational concepts are missing. Every hour spent on complex topics without fixing the root gap is wasted time. The problem is invisible to the student — they do not know what they do not know.

**Four — No retrieval practice.** Students never test themselves without help. The brain never practises recalling information under pressure. On exam day they freeze on material they genuinely knew.

**Five — Burnout and anxiety.** Accumulated study pressure without detection or response. Students push through exhaustion, performance degrades, and they break — often weeks before the exam that defines years of their life. No tool today detects this or responds to it.

The fragmentation makes all five worse. 92% of students now use generative AI. Most default to ChatGPT. They get answers but skip the retrieval practice that actually improves scores. 60% worry it is harming their critical thinking. They know it is making them passive. They want something different. They just do not have it yet.

Cognition OS is that something different.

**The chat never resets.** Persistent memory saved to Supabase. Last 50 messages loaded on every open. Student comes back tomorrow and the conversation continues exactly where it left off. No amnesia. No starting over. Ever.

**The chat is connected to every module.** When any module updates — a flashcard reviewed, a session completed, a mastery level changed, a mock test analysed — the chat knows immediately and references it naturally.

**How the chat handles different intents:**

- *"explain Carnot cycles"* — knows from history this concept has been confused before. Explains it differently this time. Uses a personal analogy. Ends with a real exam question.

- *"I just gave a mock test"* — upload it. Autopsy runs. Recoverable score, mistake breakdown, sprint plan all inside the conversation.
- *"I'm scared I won't make it"* — responds with real data about their progress, not generic motivation. "Your last three mocks show Biology improving — 280 to 310 to 340. The trajectory is good. Here is what to focus on this week."

## The Six Modules — All Invisible, All Connected

The student never sees module names. They never navigate to separate pages. They just talk to the chat and experience a product that gets eerily accurate about their needs over time. Underneath, six engines run continuously and share one student model.

### MIND — AI Tutor

The heart of the product. Does not just answer — actively builds understanding through the right combination of explanation and challenge depending on the student's intent.

**Two modes, selected automatically:**

*Doubt Mode* — student has a quick question. Answer it immediately, completely, better than ChatGPT because it is shaped by their exam and their history. Then one sharp follow-up question at the end to confirm understanding. Fast. Direct. Satisfying.

*Learning Mode* — student explicitly wants to be tested, or the OS detects they are in a study session. Socratic method activates. Forces retrieval. Finds the fracture point. Uses personal analogies. Minimum 8 to 10 exchanges before a concept is considered covered. Ends with a real exam question. References past mistakes explicitly.

**How MIND personalises every response:**

Every response built from the student's complete profile — uploaded material, mistake history, weak areas, mastery levels, current emotional state, learning style, exam type. No generic answers. Ever.

### COMMAND — Daily Mission Engine

Synthesises everything overnight — weak areas from ATLAS, due flashcards from MEMORY, recent mistakes from AUTOPSY, exam countdown, cognitive load from PULSE — into one focused session card. Student never asks "what should I study today?" The answer is already there. Computed overnight. Waiting in the morning.

### AUTOPSY — Mock Test Intelligence

The viral feature. Upload any test — PDF, photo, scanned OMR sheet, manual entry. AI classifies every wrong answer by root cause: conceptual gap, calculation error, silly mistake, time pressure, or anxiety blank.

Then it shows the number that changes everything:

> *"You scored 480. But 64 of your lost marks came from silly mistakes and time pressure — not knowledge gaps. Those marks are already yours. Here are three specific things to fix this week to cross 540."*

That message will be screenshotted. That message will be shared. That is the viral moment that drives word of mouth. No general AI can produce this. Only a product that knows this specific student's entire history can.

### ATLAS — Knowledge Graph

A living map of everything the student knows and does not know across their entire syllabus. Six-tier mastery per concept — Not Started, Exposed, Developing, Proficient, Mastered, Automated. ATLAS understands prerequisite relationships. When it sees a weak spot it traces the prerequisite chain backward and shows exactly where the root gap lives.

The social moment — a beautiful visual showing exact knowledge state across the entire syllabus. "My NEET knowledge map after 6 months of Cognition OS" will become a real social media post category. Students will share this. That is organic acquisition.

### MEMORY — FSRS-5 Spaced Repetition Engine

The most mathematically advanced spaced repetition algorithm in existence. Calculates precisely when each concept is about to fall below retention threshold. Reviews it at exactly that moment.

Student never creates flashcards. MEMORY creates them automatically from uploaded material. AUTOPSY creates them from mock test mistakes. MIND creates them when a gap is detected mid-session. The whole system feeds MEMORY. Student just reviews. Result — 90% retention of everything studied with minimum review time.

### PULSE — Mental State Engine

The feature no competitor has. Reads behavioural signals continuously — typing patterns, session length, drop-off rates, accuracy trends, response length. Constructs a continuous model of the student's cognitive and emotional state.

When it detects focus and momentum — pushes harder. When it detects stress — simplifies and reassures. When it detects early burnout — reduces daily target before the student breaks.

Burnout is one of the top reasons serious students fail. Not lack of knowledge. Not lack of effort. Burnout. PULSE is the first learning tool that treats students as humans not test-taking machines.

## The Architecture

- **Next.js 15 App Router** — server side rendering, streaming responses, edge deployment. Fast everywhere in the world.
- **Supabase with PostgreSQL and pgvector** — every concept, mistake, flashcard, session, and conversation message stored in RLS-protected environments. pgvector enables semantic search for context retrieval.
- **Gemini API** — primary AI model via API. Function calling for Orchestrator routing. Multi-agent orchestration for complex tasks. No proprietary model development — pure product layer on top of the API.
- **FSRS-5** — gold standard spaced repetition. 90% target retention. Memory decay computed per card.
- **Google text-embedding-004** — 768-dimensional semantic embeddings for every concept and every chunk of uploaded material.
- **Zustand** — chat history survives navigation. Never resets between tabs.
- **Supabase Realtime** — event bus connecting all six modules. When any module updates, chat knows immediately.

**The Orchestrator Architecture:**

Two-tier AI system. Tier one — The Orchestrator. Reads intent. Routes to the correct engine invisibly using Gemini function calling. Tier two — Worker Engines. Specialised agents activated only when called. The Planner. The Tutor. The Autopsy Engine. The Flashcard Engine. The Analytics Engine. Student never sees this routing. They just talk.
